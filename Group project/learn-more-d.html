Disinformation

New Form of Disinformation

Disinformation exists in propaganda and malicious content such as hoaxes and phishing. The contents mainly appear in the form of deceptive titles, false in-text content, malicious distortion of picture facts, etc.

A synthetic media Deepfake has gained attention since 2017. With the portmanteau of "Deep learning" and "Fake", AI deep learning technology can synthesize images, videos, or even sounds of a person. The most common application is to superimpose an existing image or video onto the target image or video. Through the Generative Adversarial Network, an artificial human face is created, and the manipulated human actions are optimized. Both are under high authenticity. People's faces, especially celebrities', can easily be used without their knowledge. Pornographic videos are the major abuse of Deepfake. It may also be used to create authentic imposter images or videos to harm celebrities. Therefore, it can become a carrier of disinformation.

Types of Disinformation

  • Imposter Content: Genuine sources are being impersonated
    E.g. A resigned PepsiCo executive revealed, "The recipe of Pepsi Cola is plundered from Coca-Cola!"
  • Fabricated Content: A 100% fake news content is designed to deceive and do harm
    E.g. 8 Billion people were shocked! The Coca-Cola Company has acquired PepsiCo 10 years ago!
  • False Context: Genuine content is shared with false contextual information
    E.g. PepsiCo's market value is expected to surpass Coca-Cola's in the future. Coca-Cola is undergoing liquidation.
  • Manipulated Content: Genuine information or imagery is manipulated to deceive.
    E.g. Pepsi's ad openly provokes Coca-Cola, threatening to replace Coke!

Purposes for Creating Disinformation

  • Financial: To earn profit from disinformation
  • Political: To discredit a political celebrity, especially in the election process, and influence public opinions
  • Social: To connect and enhance certain groups that aggravate the echo chamber
  • Psychological: To affect people's beliefs
