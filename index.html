About Us

EnlightEye is the a place discussing rumors, fake news, misinformation, and disinformation with the goal of enlightening researchers on the subject and dispelling common misconceptions about the subjects covered. We strive to provide the knowledge you require and raise awareness about these issues.

Overview

With the rapid growth of online social networks, it has become one of the main sources for people to receive news and information. According to the Digital News Report 2023 from the Reuters Institute for the Study of Journalism, only 22% of people prefer to directly use news websites or apps as the main way of receiving news. While most young adults around the globe prefer social media access. However, unlike professional publication sites such as the New York Times, the credibility of the information is doubted. Social media information has strong interactivity, fast dissemination speed, low production and use threshold, and wide coverage. The gatekeeping level for authenticity and accuracy is relatively low. Thus, it is important for us to determine, distinguish, and filter the information we receive in our daily lives.

Rumors

Rumors are pieces of information or stories that are circulated among people, often through words of mouth or via communication channels such as social media. Rumors typically involve unverified and unconfirmed information that may be exaggerated. They spread quickly and widely. Potentially influence public opinion, create confusion and impact people involved.

Fake News

With the growth of social media, information is flooded nowadays. Fake news, defined as the purposeful presentation of false or misleading statements as news, where these are misleading by design by Gelfert (2018). Both Zhang & Ghorbani (2020) and Lazer et al. (2018) agree that fake news intends to spread misleading information to the public.

Misinformation

Misinformation is incorrect or misleading information. Sometimes all it takes is a simple reporting error. At other times, clickbait headlines and facts taken out of context are used to intentionally inflate disinformation content, making it more difficult to ignore and trying to convince readers of false information. At first, definitions of disinformation focused on claims that were obviously untrue, incorrect or lacking in factual accuracy.

Online misinformation is spreading. Moreover, with the help of new technologies, anyone may now significantly alter images and movies to create a reality that doesn't actually exist.

Disinformation

Disinformation is fake and false information that is shared deliberately and intentionally to harm a person, social group, organization, or country (Wardle & Derakhshan, 2017; Aïmeur et al., 2023). In other words, disinformation is shared intentionally to mislead the readers. It is a type of fake news and information disorder.

Why We Should Care

Personal decision Making

Accurate information is needed for making decisions in our lives such as personal relationships, financial matters, and health choices. Rumors, fake news, misinformation, and disinformation may mislead us to make poor and wrong discussions, leading to wrong consequences.

Reputation

False information and rumors may harm the reputation and identity. In the age of social media and online platforms, misinformation can quickly spread to others and damage the image of us, affecting our personal relationship and overall social standing.

Social Cohesion

Rumors, fake news, misinformation, and disinformation may contribute to social division in society. When false information is spread out, it will create bias and deepen ideological divides and create conflicts between different groups. Leading to social fragmentation.

Trust in institutions

Media, government, and organization need to build trust towards the public. However, when people receive false and misleading information from Media, government, and organization, skepticism and confidence. This can be a long term implication for societal stability and cooperation.

How to Prevent?

Critical mindset

Be critical and rational to the content. Do not be influenced by the emotional appeal easily.

Be skeptical of suspicious fake news

Stay alert to the source, record of publication as well as stance, especially with political-related news.

Check the photos

Fake news often employed Photoshopped or AI-generated photographs. Look for the defect or use Google Reverse Image search to detect past usage.

Remove filtered favorite information

Social media suggests material based on a user's preferences and surfing history. Removing the personalized option exposes people to varied perspectives and prevents filter bubbles and echo chambers.

Be aware of reposting or forwarding

Do not repost the message to prevent it from further expansion when you cannot determine the authenticity.

Use a fact-checking site

Take the initiative to find out the truth! Sites (PolitiFact, FactCheck.org, Snopes, BBC Reality Check, Washington Post Fact Checker)
