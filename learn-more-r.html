False Rumors of Explosion at White House

On 23 April, 2013, there was a fake tweet telling that explosions at the White House had injured Barack Obama, the president of the US, leading to intense financial market impact. Some traders sold S&P futures and purchased Treasury 10-year futures. Within a few minutes, there was a flurry of activity, until the Associated Press tweeted that its account had been hacked.

KFC Chicken are genetically engineered

In 2016, there is a story mentioning that KFC began to inject their livestock full of hormones and altered them into no breaks and feathers, making them grow multiple wings. This allows KFC to save money and raise the size of their chickens. Then, KFC initiated a legal action against the companies, accusing them of slander, and emerged victorious in the lawsuit. Also, KFC issued a press release to public to clarify their suppliers are well known companies such as Pilgrim’s Pride and Tyson’s.

Li Ka-Shing is suspected of being seriously ill and writes a sunset ballad by himself

Some articles claiming that Li Ka-Shing was seriously ill were circulated online. Domestic media such as ‘Central Agency’ and ‘Apple Daily’ highlight keywords such as ‘Li Ka-Shing’ and ‘serious ill’. This created the meaning that the retirement of Li Ka-Shing was due to his illness. However, some people found that the article actually combined the content from multiple online articles.

Misconception of rumors

  • Confirmation bias People usually are more willing to accept information that aligns with their existing beliefs or concepts. This affects their acceptance of rumors in order to confirm their bias, even if they lack evidence or credibility.
  • Exaggeration and distortion Exaggeration and distortion of facts are usually involved in rumors. While people may involuntarily amplify or misinterpret the information when sharing rumors. Also, rumors may evoke strong emotional responses such as fear and disgust. Leading to cloud judgment and exaggeration of facts and creating a misconception to others.
